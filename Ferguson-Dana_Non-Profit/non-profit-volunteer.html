Restoration Volunteers

Human Hands are the Tools of Restoration

Add glove, shovels, and brush axes to human hands, and you have the strongest dune restoration tool around. Within months of initially removing invasive plants, volunteers witness native plants re-sprouting on their own. Our methods of removal have minimal impacts to the dunes, and thus native plants are able to repopulate the dunes on their own.

Education Volunteers

Bay to Dunes Educator

Be a volunteer for the Bay to Dunes Education Program. Field trip guides are needed to take small groups of 3rd-6th graders into the dunes and out to the bay on Tuesdays and Fridays from 9 a.m. to 1:30 p.m. Through hands-on activities, field trip guides teach kids about native dunes plants, habitat restoration, salt marsh ecology, animal tracking, birdwatching, freshwater invertebrates, dune geology, and human history. A full training is provided, call 707-444-1397 or email suzie@friendsofthedunes.org for more information.

Ocean Day Class Leader

Volunteer to be a class leader for Ocean Day on June 7th, 2012. Ocean Day is a large event with about 1,000 students participating! We need class leaders to guide school groups in habitat restoration and beach clean-up activities. A training is provided, and volunteers receive a free t-shirt! Contact Suzie at 707-444-1397 or suzie@friendsofthedunes.org for more information.
